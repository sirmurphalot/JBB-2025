By Your Own Hand: a Very Basic Drawing Tutorial

Presenter: Kristi Kong is an adopted child of Murph’s from undergrad, Kristi is a self-employed illustrator in Pennsylvania. They come from an intersectional background as a queer Taiwanese-American, which has most likely contributed to their diverse experience in both the sciences and the arts, the cerebral and the athletic, and their love for both cats and dogs (cats a little more). Kristi hopes to have a fun, stress-free drawing session with everyone at the JBB 2025!

Let’s draw a hand together! The human hand is universally recognized as one of the most intimidating subjects to draw due to its complex structure and organic movement. I will be demystifying the process of drawing The Manus by simplifying it into three steps: form, contour, and shading. Guiding JBB 2025 attendants through the fundamentals of pencil drawing in this 20-minute workshop, I will include a live tutorial, completely subjective opinions on how to approach a drawing, and some possible philosophical digressions on the importance of making space for human-made art. No prior experience needed or expected! The ultimate goal of this session will be creating by your own hand.

Please bring 1 pencil and 1 completely blank sheet of paper for this talk
