Ancient and Medieval Graffiti and Drawings

Presenter: Rocky is a very, very amateur historian who gets into internet deep-dives from time to time. They get hella excited about castle ruins, historic train depots, special collection libraries, and how human imprints and attitudes survive the test of time.

Abstract: A comedic PowerPoint presentation on a selection of ancient and medieval graffiti and drawings, intended to inform audience members of the various ways some people expressed themselves on paper (or on walls), and how humankind has always been silly. Topics will cover Pompeiian graffiti, the doodles of 7-year-old Onfim from 13th century Novgorod, Russia, and the banal and inconsiderate graffiti of Viking travelers. Powerpoint will contain illustrations and photographs of historic sites and artifacts from archival media troves. If materials can be found, birchbark and styluses may be handed to conference participants for an interactive experience.

Conference Room 72, 9:00pm
